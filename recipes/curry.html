Chicken Curry

Description

Chicken curry is a dish popular in South-East Asia. It generally consists of chicken served with a sauce that is onion and tomato based, generally flavoured with ginger, garlic, chilli and having a variety of spices added to it such as coriander, turmeric, cumin and cinnamon.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil
  • 1 and a 1/2 cups chopped onion
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup plain yoghurt
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala

Steps:

  1. Sprinkle the chicken breasts with 2 teaspoons salt.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat; partially cook the chicken in the hot oil in batches until completely browned on all sides. Transfer browned chicken breasts to a plate and set aside.
  3. Reduce the heat to medium and add onion, garlic, and ginger to the oil remaining in the skillet. Cook and stir until onion turns soft, 5 to 8 minutes. Add curry powder, cumin, turmeric, coriander, and 1 tablespoon of water into the onion mixture and stir; allow to heat together for about 1 minute while stirring. Add tomatoes, yogurt, and 1 teaspoon salt to the mixture; stir to combine.
  4. Return chicken breast to the skillet along with any juices on the plate. Pour in 1/2 cup water and bring to a boil, turning the chicken to coat with the sauce. Sprinkle 1 tablespoon garam masala over the chicken.
  5. Cover the skillet and simmer until chicken breasts are no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, about 20 minutes.
